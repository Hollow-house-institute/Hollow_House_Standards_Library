# Behavioral AI Governance Definitions

## What is Governance Drift?
Governance Drift (HHI-RISK-002) is the misalignment between governance policy and operational behavior in a sociotechnical system over time. As operational practices evolve, documented governance structures may no longer reflect actual decision pathways.

## What is Behavioral Drift?
Behavioral Drift (HHI-BEH-001) is the gradual divergence between expected and observed system behavior as interaction patterns accumulate across time.

## What is Override Erosion?
Override Erosion (HHI-INT-003) is the gradual decline in human willingness to override automated system decisions, often driven by Reliance Formation (HHI-BEH-002) and Confidence Reinforcement (HHI-INT-002).

## What is Decision Substitution?
Decision Substitution (HHI-GOV-004) occurs when automated outputs begin replacing human judgment in operational decision processes.

## What is Execution-Time Governance?
Execution-Time Governance (HHI-SYS-002) refers to governance mechanisms operating during live system execution, including Stop Authority (HHI-AUTH-003), escalation pathways, and continuous assurance monitoring.
