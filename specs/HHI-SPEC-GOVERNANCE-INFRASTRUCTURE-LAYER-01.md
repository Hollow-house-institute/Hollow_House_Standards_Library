## 1. Real System Behavior Observation
A deployed AI system begins influencing operational decisions across multiple teams. Over time, the system’s outputs shape workflows, override patterns, and escalation behavior—even though no formal policy change has occurred.

## 2. Canonical Governance Term
**Governance Infrastructure Layer**  
**Identifier:** HHI-GOV-020

## 3. Exact Glossary Definition
**Governance Infrastructure Layer** — "Structural components enforcing governance rules."

## 4. Behavioral Impact
When system behavior begins shaping organizational behavior, the Governance Infrastructure Layer determines whether:

- Decision Boundaries remain intact  
- Authority Drift is detected  
- Escalation pathways function  
- Longitudinal Risk is monitored  
- Accountability remains traceable  
- Behavioral Drift is corrected before it compounds  

Without a functioning Governance Infrastructure Layer, organizations experience:

- Governance Drift  
- Governance Illusion  
- Normalization of Workarounds  
- Post-Hoc Governance  
- Override Erosion  

This layer is where **Execution-Time Governance** becomes enforceable rather than aspirational.
