# HHI Governance Terms

Version: v1.3.0  
Canonical Terms: 41  
Maintained by: Hollow House Institute

This document provides the canonical definitions for governance terminology used in the HHI Governance Glossary.

Identifiers are defined in TERM_INDEX.md and remain stable across versions.

---

## AUTH — Authority and Decision Control

HHI-AUTH-001 — Authority  
The formally assigned right to make, override, pause, or stop decisions during execution.

HHI-AUTH-002 — Decision Boundary  
The explicit point at which authority transfers between actors during execution.

HHI-AUTH-003 — Stop Authority  
A role-bound capability to halt system operation when governance thresholds are exceeded.

HHI-AUTH-004 — Human-in-the-Loop  
Governance mechanism requiring human oversight in decision processes.

HHI-AUTH-005 — Escalation  
Transfer of decision authority to higher roles when risk thresholds are reached.

HHI-AUTH-006 — Escalation Decay  
Reduction in the effectiveness or use of escalation pathways over time.

HHI-AUTH-007 — Escalation Suppression  
Condition where escalation pathways exist but are not used.

HHI-AUTH-008 — Authority Persistence  
Continuation of decision authority beyond the original operational conditions.

## BEH — Behavioral Governance Foundations

HHI-BEH-001 — Behavioral Drift  
Gradual divergence between expected system behavior and observed behavior over time.

HHI-BEH-002 — Reliance Formation  
Process through which individuals begin depending on system outputs.

HHI-BEH-003 — Governance as Infrastructure  
Governance structures that accumulate through repeated behavioral patterns.

HHI-BEH-004 — Post-Hoc Governance  
Governance actions applied only after failures or outcomes are questioned.

HHI-BEH-005 — Continuous Assurance  
Ongoing monitoring of governance performance rather than periodic audits.

HHI-BEH-006 — Longitudinal Accountability  
Traceability of authority and responsibility across time.

---

## GOV — Governance Mechanisms and Accountability

HHI-GOV-001 — Accountability  
Binding outcomes to named roles with authority prior to execution.

HHI-GOV-002 — Accountability Diffusion  
Loss of clear responsibility caused by distributed decision processes.

HHI-GOV-003 — Responsibility Binding  
Mechanism linking authority to responsible actors.

HHI-GOV-004 — Decision Substitution  
Replacement of human decision-making with automated system outputs.

---

## RISK — Longitudinal Risk and Failure Dynamics

HHI-RISK-001 — Governance Lag  
Delay between behavioral change and governance response.

HHI-RISK-002 — Governance Drift  
Misalignment between governance policy and operational behavior.

HHI-RISK-003 — Longitudinal Risk  
Risk emerging through repeated behavioral patterns over time.

HHI-RISK-004 — Behavioral Accumulation  
Compounding effect of repeated actions reshaping system behavior.

HHI-RISK-005 — Governance Failure  
Breakdown of governance mechanisms controlling system behavior.

HHI-RISK-006 — Authority Drift  
Gradual shift in decision authority away from intended structures.

HHI-RISK-007 — Intervention Threshold  
Point where governance intervention becomes necessary.

---

## INT — Human–System Interaction Dynamics

HHI-INT-001 — Judgment Externalization  
Transfer of interpretive responsibility from humans to automated systems.

HHI-INT-002 — Confidence Reinforcement  
Feedback loop where system responses increase user confidence.

HHI-INT-003 — Override Erosion  
Decline in willingness to override automated decisions.

HHI-INT-004 — Normalization of Workarounds  
Temporary fixes gradually becoming accepted operational practices.

HHI-INT-005 — Governance Illusion  
Perception of governance control without effective enforcement.

HHI-MET-003 — Governance Stability Index (GSI)
