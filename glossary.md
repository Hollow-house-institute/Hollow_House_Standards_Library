# HHI Governance Glossary

## Behavioral Drift

Gradual divergence between expected system behavior and observed behavior over time.

## Reliance Formation

Process by which individuals or organizations begin depending on system outputs regardless of governance approval.

## Governance as Infrastructure

Repeated behavioral patterns embedded within operational systems.

## Post-Hoc Governance

Governance actions occurring only after outcomes are questioned or failures occur.

## Continuous Assurance

Continuous monitoring of governance performance rather than periodic audits.

## Longitudinal Accountability

Traceability of responsibility and decision authority across time.

## Authority

Formally assigned right to make decisions within a system.

## Decision Boundary

Point where decision authority transfers between actors.

## Stop Authority

Capability to halt system operations when governance thresholds are exceeded.

## Human-in-the-Loop

Governance mechanism requiring human oversight in decision processes.

## Escalation

Transfer of decisions to higher authority when risk thresholds are met.

## Escalation Decay

Reduction in effectiveness or use of escalation pathways over time.

## Escalation Suppression

Condition where escalation pathways exist but are not used.

## Authority Persistence

Continuation of decision authority beyond original conditions.

## Accountability

Assignment of responsibility for decisions and outcomes.

## Accountability Diffusion

Loss of clear responsibility due to distributed decision processes.

## Responsibility Binding

Mechanism linking authority to responsible actors.

## Decision Substitution

Replacement of human decision-making with automated outputs.

## Governance Lag

Delay between behavioral change and governance response.

## Governance Drift

Gradual misalignment between governance policy and operational behavior.

## Longitudinal Risk

Risk emerging from cumulative behavior over time.

## Behavioral Accumulation

Compounding effect of repeated actions reshaping system behavior.

## Governance Failure

Breakdown of governance mechanisms controlling system behavior.

## Authority Drift

Gradual shift in decision authority away from intended structures.

## Intervention Threshold

Point where governance intervention becomes necessary.

## Judgment Externalization

Transfer of decision reasoning from humans to external systems.

## Confidence Reinforcement

Feedback loop where system responses increase user confidence.

## Override Erosion

Decline in willingness to override automated decisions.

## Normalization of Workarounds

Temporary fixes becoming permanent operational practices.

## Governance Illusion

Perception of governance control without effective enforcement.

## Language Symmetry Score

Metric evaluating alignment between outputs and governance terminology.

## Relational Rhythm Index

Measure of stability in human-system interaction patterns.

## Governance Stability Index

Indicator of long-term governance consistency.

## Authority Alignment Score

Metric measuring alignment between authority and governance design.

## Relational Health Dashboard

Monitoring interface for human-system interaction dynamics.

## Governance Telemetry

Operational signals measuring governance performance.

## Interaction Trace

Recorded sequence of human-system interactions.

## Sociotechnical System

System composed of human, technical, and organizational components.

## Execution-Time Governance

Governance mechanisms operating during system execution.

## Governance Infrastructure Layer

Structural elements enforcing governance rules.

## Governance Surface

Observable layer where governance interactions occur.
