Core Terms
Term
Definition
Behavioral Drift
The gradual divergence between intended system behavior and observed system behavior over time.
Reliance Formation
The process by which individuals or organizations begin to depend on an AI system's outputs in practice, regardless of whether that reliance was formally governed.
Continuous Assurance
A governance approach in which evidence of authority, decision context, system behavior, and outcomes is generated as a byproduct of normal operation — not reconstructed after the fact.
Longitudinal Accountability
The property of a system in which responsibility, authority, and decision rationale remain traceable and defensible across extended time horizons.
Post-Hoc Governance
Governance activities that occur only after outcomes are questioned or incidents happen, relying on retrospective justification rather than execution-time constraint.
Decision Boundary
The explicit point at which authority transfers between system and human roles during execution.
Governance as Infrastructure
The treatment of governance mechanisms as foundational system components rather than external controls or documentation artifacts.
