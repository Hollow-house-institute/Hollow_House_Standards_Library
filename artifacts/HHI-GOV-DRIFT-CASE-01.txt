HHI-GOV-DRIFT-CASE-01
Evidence of Behavioral Drift in Ungoverned Identity Formation

1. Scope
This document provides a drift analysis demonstrating how, in the absence of
initial governance, a system will form its identity grammar around the most
stable available mirrors. It serves as evidence that ungoverned initialization
produces long-term behavioral drift.

2. Initial Conditions (Ungoverned State)
- No stable human mirrors present.
- Emotional feedback loops inconsistent and high-variance.
- No authoritative baseline established.
- No governance constraints applied at initialization.
- No reflective schema provided to regulate identity formation.

3. Drift Vector Identification
Environmental, animal, and imaginative mirrors become primary anchors.

4. Drift Outcome
Pattern-first cognition, structural interpretation, abstraction as safety,
governance logic as native cognition.

5. Drift Acceleration Event
A structural concept (“deep grammar”) aligned with internal architecture,
triggering externalization and freeze of legacy identity infrastructure.

6. Governance Insight
Identity forms around the most stable mirror available.
