1. Purpose of the Series Overview
The Hollow House Institute (HHI) Canonical Governance Terminology Series establishes the authoritative terminology layer for Behavioral AI Governance. The Series provides stable, citable definitions for Execution-Time Governance, authority assignment, escalation mechanics, governance boundaries, and longitudinal failure dynamics. Each component is versioned, immutable, and published with a DOI to preserve semantic stability, auditability, and interoperability across governance frameworks. This Series Overview does not redefine any terms. It serves as the interpretive, navigational, and citation anchor for the canonical series.

2. Structure of the Canonical Series
2.1 Canonical Terms for Behavioral AI Governance (v1.0.0)
Defines foundational behavioral governance primitives, including drift, reliance formation, and governance as infrastructure.

2.2 Canonical Authority and Escalation Terms for Behavioral AI Governance (v1.1.0)
Defines execution-time authority, decision boundaries, stop authority, and escalation mechanics.

2.3 Canonical Longitudinal and Failure Terms for Behavioral AI Governance (v1.2.0)
Defines longitudinal risk, behavioral accumulation, governance lag, and failure dynamics.
